Well-posed Learning Problems

A computer program is said to learn from experience E with respect to some class of tasks T and performance measure P, if its peformance at tasks in T, as measured by P, improves with experience E.

 Choosing the Training experience

 - The type of feedback: Whether the training experience provides direct or indirect feedback on the system's choices.
 - The degree of control: How much the learner controls the sequence of training examples it receives
 - The representativeness: How well the training examples represent the actual distribution of problems the system will when its final performance is evaluated.
